    Leah

    Leah (first wife of Jacob)

    Leah was the first wife of Jacob and the mother of six of his twelve sons, who became the ancestors of six of the twelve tribes of Israel. She was the daughter of Laban and the older sister of Rachel. Leah's story is one of love, rivalry, and faithfulness, as she bore children to Jacob while Rachel, whom he loved more, remained barren for a time. Despite the challenges she faced, Leah is remembered for her significant role in the lineage of the Israelite people, particularly as the mother of Judah, from whom the Davidic line would come.

    Associations

    • Associated with Ruth (Ruth 4:11)
    • Child of Laban (Genesis 29:16)
    • Leader of Zilpah (Genesis 29:24)
    • Married to Jacob (Genesis 29:23)
    • Parent of Reuben (Genesis 29:32), Simeon (Genesis 29:33), Levi (Genesis 29:34), Judah (Genesis 29:35), Issachar (Genesis 30:18), Zebulun (Genesis 30:20), Dinah (Genesis 30:21), Dinah (Genesis 34:1)
